Examination Criteria

Examinee prompt

Done

Actor prompt

Done

  1. Introduces self appropriately to mother and child and obtains consent. Puts patient at ease.


  2. Takes an appropriate history of the presenting symptom: Duration of diarrhoea, how many nappies he used, mucus, blood, pus, fat present


  3. Inquires about other presenting symptoms - Vomiting, fever/sweats, sleepiness, mood


  4. Inquires about risk factors for dehydration - diminished food and fluid intake, what food has been consumed, urine output


  5. Enquires about precipitating factors: Infected family members/childcare, recent antibiotic use


  6. Takes an accurate past medical history (previous episodes, concurrent illnesses, past hospitalisations/surgery, allergies, immunisations)


  7. Inquires (birefly) about details of birth, whether he has been on trac, developmentally to date


  8. Social history (occupation of parents, living circumstances, family and social supports)


  9. Family history (common hereditary diseases, health of parents, health of siblings/children)


  10. Gives feedback to the patient at various points in the interview, and clarifies points made


  11. Good transition from one part of the interview to the next


  12. Summarises history back to the examiner
