Examinee prompt

Done

Actor prompt

Done

  1. Introduces self appropriately


  2. Takes appropriate history of presenting symptom (min 5/8): site, onset, character, radiation, aggravating factors, relieving factors, time course, assesses severity of pain (grading or interference with activities)


  3. Appropriate history of associated symptoms (must ask about fever/malaise, and at last one other): jaundice, pruritis, change in urine/faeces, fever, malaise, vomiting/nausea, change in bowel habits


  4. Takes an accurate past medical history


  5. Inquires about medications and drug allergies


  6. Family history


  7. Social history: alcohol, smoking and at least occupational history or marital status


  8. Can indicate likely diagnosis and give information from hisstory to support this: GALLSTONES suspected due to fatty foods, location of pain


  9. Identification of at least one differential and how it is distingushed on history or examination: kidney stones, cholecystitis, pancreatitis, ulcer disease, hepatitis and appendicitis. Investigate using rectal exam, faecal occult blood test, liver function test, bilirubin, amylase, lipase and abdominal ultrasound


  10. Conducts a well-structured history


  11. Appropriate use of open and closed questions. Gives feedback to patient and clarifies information throughout interview
