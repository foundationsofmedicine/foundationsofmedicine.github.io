Actor prompt

Done

  1. Introduces self appropriately and establishes a rapport with the patient. 


  2. Positions patients correctly, removes appropriate garments (sitting on edge of bed and undressed from waist upwards). 


  3. General inspection - sputum, cough, respiratory rate, and use of accessory muscles. 


  4. Hands (names 3 or 4) - clubbing, cyanosis, nicotine stains, wasting/weakness (abduction/adduction), wrist tenderness (HPOA), pulse and flapping tremor. 


  5. Face - eyes (Horner’s), mouth (cyanosis), and voice(hoarseness)


  6. Trachea - midline and tracheal tug


  7. Inspection of chest - respiratory rate, accessory muscles of respiration, shape of chest and spine, expansion, scars, and prominent veins (direction of flow). 


  8. Palpate - cervical lymph nodes, supraclavicular lymph nodes, expansion and vocal fremitus. 


  9. Percuss - patients should be asked to bring their elbows together to get the scapulae out of the way, supraclavicular region, and back/axillae.


  10. Auscultate - patients should be asked to breathe through the mouth, breath sounds, adventitious sounds and vocal resonance. A logical pattern of auscultation should be used. Sides should be compared. 


  11. Pemberton’s Sign - does not need to wait the full minute. 


  12. Mentions cardiovascular exam, JVP, FVC and temperature.


  13. Additional Question: What is Pemberton’s Sign?


  14. Additional Question: What is a likely cause of Horner’s syndrome in a respiratory patient?
