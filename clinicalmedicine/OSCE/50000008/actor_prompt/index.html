Presenting Complaint
My husband wanted me to see a doctor because I’ve been getting more headaches and it has been making it difficult for me to take care of the children at times.

I’ve suffered from headaches since I was a teenager.

The first one occurred when I was about 18 years old.

I went to the doctor when I was 20 years old and he told me that I suffered from migraines.

Normally I would get about 4 migraines a year but over the last 3-4 months I’ve had a migraine every 2 weeks.

When a migraine begins, the first thing that happens is that I get a spreading front of tingling followed by numbness. This lasts 20-30 minutes and moves from one part of the body to another. For instance, it may move from my hand to my face.

There is no weakness.

No muscle twitching.

Following the numbness, I get a throbbing, pounding, painful headache that normally lasts for 6-8 hours.

The headache is usually on one side of my head. Hard to say exactly where.

The pain is only in my head and not anywhere else.

When I get the headache, I get really sensitive to light and have to squint in daylight.

I normally get really badly nauseous and usually vomit at some point during the headache.

I feel better if I lie in a darkened room and try to sleep until the headache is gone.

I've never taken any medication while I’m having the migraine.

A doctor once put me on a dry called pizotifen to help stop me getting migraines but it made me put on heaps of weight so I stopped taking it.

Chocolate and red wine seem to make me get more migraines so I don’t eat chocolate or drink red wine anymore.

Strangely, I seemed to get less migraines when I was pregnant with my children.

Past Medical History
No significant medical illnesses.

I've only ever been in hospital to give birth to my children: 3 vaginal, 1 caesarean.

No high blood pressure.

No diabetes.

Medications
OCP.

Aspirin when I get a migraine.

Social History
I don’t smoke. I used to smoke 20 cigarettes a day from when I was about 16 years old to when I was 24. I stopped when I became pregnant with my first child

I don't drink anymore just in case it causes a migraine.

I have four children aged 3, 4, 9 and 11.

Family History
Both parents are still alive. Dad has high blood pressure and mum is well other than some arthritis in her knees.

My sister (32) suffers from migraines as well.
