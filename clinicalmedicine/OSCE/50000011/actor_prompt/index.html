Patient Task
History of Presenting Complaint
Presented to GP a age 56 with increasing difficulty using his right hand while brushing teeth, writing etc.

His wife had noticed a mild tremor in his hands while sitting quietly.

He had been complaining that he felt as though he were ‘slowing down’ but was only 56! It was getting more difficult to get out of his chair and physically get going each day.

People had been commenting that he seemed a little unsteady on his feet.

He had also been finding it increasingly difficult to sleep.

He had experimented with heroin in the past- a few of his mates being involved in making heroin.

Disease progressed to involve both sides but still asymmetrical.

Handwriting now tiny.

Increasing rigidity.

Lack of facial expression.

Quiet voice.

Feels dizzy on standing but does not have any problems with urination, defaecation or impotence.

Past Medical History
Nil

Medications
L-DOPA and carbidopa (but efficacy has decreased as symptoms have progressed).

Side effects of medication include nausea and vomiting (initially, but improved with dose adjustment), disturbed dreams, on/off phenomena (freezes from time to time) and dyskinesia.

Has been reading about deep brain stimulation.

Family History
Mr Fox’s father had a movement disorder but Mr Fox is unsure of what the exact diagnosis was.

Social History
Frustrated and slightly depressed as he had to give up his job in the film industry.

Lives with wife, who looks after him; financially stable but bored at home.

Difficulty with ADLs (dressing, washing, feeding, grooming, tuning in bed).

Does not smoke or drink.
