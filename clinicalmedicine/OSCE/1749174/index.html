Foundations of Medicine Clinical Medicine OSCE 1749174: Lower Back Pain History

OSCE ID: 1749174

Examinee prompt Actor prompt

Examination Criteria

Examinee prompt

Done

Actor prompt

Done

  1. Introduces self appropriately and obtains consent


  2. Takes an appropriate history of the presenting symptom: Back pain - location, onset (sudden or gradual, after trauma?), character, radiating, duration, intensity, aggravating and relieving factors.


  3. Inquires about sensory changes and muscle weakness.


  4. Inquires specificially about bladder and bowel function.


  5. Inquires about previous back injuries and pain


  6. Asks about functional impairment at work.


  7. Inquires about previous medical conditions such as cancer, osteoporosis


  8. Social history - Smoking, alcohol, recreational drugs, occupation, living circumstances, family and social supports


  9. Family history - Health of parents and siblings/cause of death


  10. Gives feedback to the patient at various points in the interview, and clarifies points made.


  11. Good transition from one part of the interview to the next. Appropriate language used.


  12. Summarises history back to the examiner


  13. 10/12 required for satisfactory result


  14. Additional question: What examinations would you perform?


  15. Additional question: What investigation would be most useful?


  16. Additional question: How could you tell on examination the difference between an S1 radiculopathy and one at L5?
